# 04-ladenheim / 02 — Stage C: reproduce the 108-network canonical set

This task previously read "Compare the reduced catalogue against the

Source: A. Morelli and M. C. Smith, *Passive Network Synthesis: An Approach
to Classification*, SIAM, 2019, Ch. 5 pp. 41-43 §5.1.

## Goal

Reproduce the reduction from the 148 essentially-distinct networks
(Stage A/B, `01-148.md`) to the 108-network canonical set — usually called
the Ladenheim catalogue — as a separately documented stage that uses
electrical-network transformations and realizability arguments, not another
pass of topological canonicalization. Do not hide these operations inside
the 148 structural signature from Stage B; 108 is a distinct, later
reduction applied on top of 148, not an alternative definition of it.

This stage depends on Stage B (`01-148.md`) being complete: there must be an
independently-reproduced 148-network set to remove 40 networks from before
this stage can be validated.

## The 40 exclusions, by category

Four categories, each to be reproduced and tested separately with its own
fixtures:

1. **8 networks**: four resistors and one reactive element (`R = 4`,
   `L + C = 1`), each realizable by a simpler network with a bilinear
   driving-point impedance.
2. **4 networks**: four elements total, each reducible to a three-element
   network by a Zobel transformation.
3. **20 networks**: five elements total, series-parallel, each reducible to
   a four-element (or smaller) network by a Zobel transformation.
4. **8 networks**: related by Cauer-Foster transformation, regularity, or
   Y-Delta relationships to an O-network, its dual, or a bridge network.

`8 + 4 + 20 + 8 = 40`, and `148 - 40 = 108`.

## Tasks

- For each of the four categories above, identify the specific networks (by
  Stage B's structural signature or representative) that the category
  removes, and record why, citing the source's argument for that category
  (bilinear realizability, Zobel transformation, Cauer-Foster/regularity/
  Y-Delta), not merely "removed by the source."
- Add a fixture or small test per category asserting that the identified
  networks are excluded and that the count for that category matches
  (8, 4, 20, 8 respectively).
- Confirm the 8-networks-with-`R=4,L+C=1` category against the diagnostic
  finding in `docs/ladenheim_benchmark.md`, where the current RICE reduced-
  topology model (under the corrected 148 scope) also produces exactly 8
  signatures at `R=4, L+C=1`. Record this as a count-level consistency
  check, not as proof that RICE's eight signatures are the same eight
  networks — that requires per-network correspondence, not just a matching
  category count.
- Confirm the resulting count is 108, with the same per-`R`/per-`L+C`
  breakdown style used in Stage B.

## Done means

- Each of the four exclusion categories is separately documented, with its
  own fixtures/tests, citing the specific electrical-network argument used.
- The 40 total exclusions and the resulting 108 count are reproduced from
  the independently-derived 148 set (Stage B), not asserted directly.
- These operations are not folded into or hidden inside the 148 structural
  signature; 108 remains a documented, separate stage on top of 148.
- Counts, enumeration results, catalogues, equivalence classes, generator
  sets, and full immittance identity are not conflated.
