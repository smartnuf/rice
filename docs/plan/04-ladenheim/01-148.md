# 04-ladenheim / 01 — Stage A/B: define and reproduce the 148 essentially-distinct networks

Status: `todo`

## Revision note (2026-07-10)

This task previously read "Enumerate and record the broader candidate set
before canonical reductions," implying 148 is a raw, pre-reduction candidate
count. That is wrong: 148 is already the result of identifying networks
related by 2-isomorphism and excluding trivially-simplifiable networks. See
`docs/ladenheim_benchmark.md` for the full reassessment. This task is split
into two stages, both currently `todo` and neither implemented.

Source: A. Morelli and M. C. Smith, *Passive Network Synthesis: An Approach
to Classification*, SIAM, 2019, Ch. 3 §3.1 p. 19.

## Stage A — define the historical 148 contract precisely (no implementation)

Goal: write down, without implementing, exactly what the 148-network set is,
so Stage B has an unambiguous target.

Known elements of the contract:

- primitive R, L, or C branches only, one primitive type per branch;
- connected two-terminal basic graphs with at most five branches;
- at most two reactive elements total (`L + C <= 2`), no independent bound on
  the number of resistors;
- networks related by 2-isomorphism (deformation, separation, series
  interchange) are identified as the same network;
- networks trivially simplifiable by same-type series/parallel connections
  are excluded from the set (not merely counted once).

Open questions to settle and record before Stage B starts (do not guess at
answers; record them as open until confirmed against the source):

- The precise definition of "deformation" and "separation" as distinct
  2-isomorphism moves, versus the informal description above.
- Whether "excluding trivially simplifiable" means rejecting any network
  containing a same-type series or parallel sub-connection outright, or only
  those reducible to a strictly smaller network of the same total scope.
- How the two unordered terminals are treated when a candidate graph has a
  nontrivial automorphism group — whether terminal orientation is ever
  distinguishing.
- Whether a distinguished port/source edge convention is used internally by
  the source's construction, and if so, whether it affects the final count
  (it should not, if terminals are genuinely unordered, but this should be
  confirmed rather than assumed).

## Stage B — reproduce 148 via a dedicated Ladenheim structural signature

Goal: implement a structural signature and enumeration specific to the
Stage A contract, kept separate from the existing RICE reduced-topology
signature (`canonical_reduced_signature`, `docs/model_decisions.md`) unless
Stage E later proves the two coincide on this scope.

This is deliberately not the same code path as `rice reduced`: 2-isomorphism
is a broader relation than the graph isomorphism plus local series/parallel
reduction that RICE implements, and the exclusion of trivially-simplifiable
networks is a candidate-generation-time modelling choice in the historical
construction, not a signature-merging rule.

Tasks:

- Implement 2-isomorphism (or the specific subset of its moves confirmed by
  Stage A) as an equivalence test or canonical form for basic two-terminal
  graphs with primitive R/L/C branches, at most five branches, at most two
  reactive elements, and no resistor bound.
- Implement the trivially-simplifiable exclusion rule as a Stage A
  confirmed it, as a generation-time filter, not a post-hoc merge.
- Produce diagnostic count tables broken down by element count and
  composition (`R`, `L+C` breakdown), not just one final integer, so
  disagreements are localised rather than hidden in a single number.
- Compare the diagnostic 149 finding in `docs/ladenheim_benchmark.md`
  (obtained from the *existing* RICE reduced-topology model under the
  corrected scope, as a stopgap before Stage B exists) against this stage's
  independently-derived count once implemented, and record any remaining
  discrepancy with a specific structural explanation, not a fudge.

## Done means

- Stage A: the 148 contract is written down precisely, with open questions
  explicitly labelled, and no implementation has been started to fit a
  target count.
- Stage B: an independent Ladenheim structural signature exists, is tested
  against Stage A's contract, and its count is reported with a per-category
  breakdown.
- Do not mark reproduction of 148 as `done` until Stage B's count matches
  148 with a structural explanation for every element, or until any
  remaining discrepancy is understood and documented, not merely tuned away.
- Counts, enumeration results, catalogues, equivalence classes, generator
  sets, and full immittance identity are not conflated.
